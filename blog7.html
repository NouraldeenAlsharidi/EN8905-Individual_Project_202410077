Sharing findings with a group at SABIC — quiz, results & reflection

Stage 5 — Social Awareness Activity

Activity Overview

To satisfy Stage 5 of this project, I organised a 30-minute lunch break session with a small group of colleagues from SABIC's ICS, Electrical, and HSE departments. The session aimed to explain SDG 13, the case for offshore wind, and the energy transition trade-offs in plain, accessible language — without assuming engineering background.

How I Explained It — Key Analogies Used

  • Gas dependency = renting a car forever: You always depend on the landlord's price. Wind = buying a car — high upfront cost but free fuel forever.
  • CO₂ as a "heat blanket": Every tonne of CO₂ adds another layer to a blanket around the Earth that we can never take off — helped people viscerally understand why 490 g/kWh matters.
  • Intermittency = sunlight through clouds: Wind doesn't stop working, it just varies — like cloud cover varies solar panels, but a battery smooths it out.
  • Bahrain's size advantage: Our shallow coastal waters (avg 30 m depth) are almost ideal for fixed-bottom offshore turbines — no deep-water engineering needed.

Ethics of the Activity

Consent: All participants signed a brief verbal consent statement; participation was voluntary with no professional consequences for declining.
Anonymity: Quiz answers were collected without names; results below are aggregated only.
Safeguarding: All participants were adults; no vulnerable groups involved.
Date & Location: 7 July 2026, SABIC ICS Meeting Room B, Jubail Industrial Complex.

Quiz Questions & Results

Q1. Which energy source produces the most CO₂ per unit of electricity generated?
Options: A) Nuclear B) Natural Gas C) Offshore Wind D) Hydropower
✓ Correct Answer: B — Natural Gas (490 g/kWh) | 90% answered correctly
Q2. What is SDG 13?
Options: A) Clean Water B) Climate Action C) Zero Hunger D) Clean Energy
✓ Correct Answer: B — Climate Action | 100% answered correctly
Q3. What is the main challenge of offshore wind compared to gas power?
Options: A) Too expensive to build B) Intermittency / variable output C) Produces too much CO₂ D) Cannot connect to the grid
✓ Correct Answer: B — Intermittency | 70% answered correctly
Q4. Approximately what percentage of Bahrain's electricity currently comes from natural gas?
Options: A) 50% B) 75% C) 98% D) 100%
✓ Correct Answer: C — 98% | 60% answered correctly
Q5. If Bahrain builds a 10-turbine offshore wind farm (8 MW each, 40% CF), approximately how much CO₂ would be avoided per year compared to gas?
Options: A) 5,000 tonnes B) 50,000 tonnes C) 137,000 tonnes D) 500,000 tonnes
✓ Correct Answer: C — ~137,000 tonnes | 50% answered correctly

Reflection on Understanding

Overall, participants showed good understanding of the core climate and SDG concepts (Qs 1–2: 90–100% correct). Understanding of technical aspects (intermittency, Q3) and local statistics (Q4) was moderate. The engineering calculation (Q5) was the most challenging, which is expected for a non-engineering audience.

Key feedback received: participants were surprised by the 98% gas dependency figure and by how much CO₂ even one small wind farm could avoid. Several expressed interest in whether SABIC could pursue renewable PPAs (Power Purchase Agreements) — a direct outcome of the awareness activity feeding back into professional action.

Stage 5 Complete: A small group of colleagues, 5-question quiz, aggregated anonymous results, verbal feedback documented, photos taken (consent obtained). Session confirmed that clear analogy-driven engineering communication is effective in building public understanding of complex energy transition issues.
